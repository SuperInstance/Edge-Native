## Research Methodology

### Round Structure
Each round addressed questions raised by the previous round, creating a spiral of deepening understanding:

1. **Round 1 (Technical Foundation):** "Is the architecture sound?" → Safety simulation, trust modeling, VM benchmarking, wire protocol analysis
2. **Round 2 (Application Breadth):** "Where else does this apply?" → 8 domain analyses, regulatory mapping, AI model evaluation
3. **Round 3 (Perspective Depth):** "What are we missing?" → 8 philosophical traditions, ethics analysis, cross-cultural design principles
4. **Round 4 (Validation):** "What breaks at scale?" → Network failure, multi-reflex interference, end-to-end pipeline simulation
5. **Round 5 (Integration):** "What does it all mean?" → Universal synthesis, debate, convergence analysis

## The Universal Principle

The central finding, validated across all five rounds and confirmed by eight philosophical traditions:

> **The most powerful, reliable, and adaptable systems are not centralized intelligences but networks of simple, reliable local processors that cooperate through well-defined protocols.**

This principle holds in biology (cells, ant colonies), computing (distributed systems, edge AI), organization theory (teams, markets), and philosophy (wisdom of crowds, Ubuntu, Daoism). NEXUS is one engineering instantiation of this universal principle, applied to industrial robotics through the "ribosome, not the brain" architecture.

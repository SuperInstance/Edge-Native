## The Convergent Truths

Across all layers of analysis — creative, technical, cultural, and research — twelve architectural truths emerged with such robustness that they survived translation through every framework:

1. **Intelligence lives in the relationship, not in any component.**
2. **No component comprehends the colony — maximal local ignorance produces emergent global intelligence.**
3. **Design for conditions, not behaviors — set the soil and the climate, and the garden grows itself.**
4. **The bytecode-environment partnership is the fundamental unit of adaptation.**
5. **Governance without a governor — the colony has no master node.**
6. **Three-timescale coordination: endocrine (slow), nervous (fast), immune (evolutionary).**
7. **Competition at the node level; cooperation at the colony level; seasonal alternation between both.**
8. **Recovery is creative reconstitution, not restoration — the colony evolves through loss.**
9. **The Jetson is seasonally modal: passive flower, active mother, partial observer.**
10. **Substrate independence: MYCELIUM's intelligence resides in relationships, not hardware.**
11. **Metabolism is a first-class observable — energy efficiency is a colony health indicator.**
12. **Colony-level emergence is a testable hypothesis — instrument it, measure it, prove or disprove it.**
